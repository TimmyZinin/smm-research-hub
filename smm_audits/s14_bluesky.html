1. Algorithm & Feed Mechanics

Bluesky's architecture is fundamentally different from any other social platform: there is no single monolithic algorithm. Instead, users choose from pluggable feed generators built on the open AT Protocol.

Critical Insight: Bluesky does NOT penalize external links. Unlike X/Twitter which suppresses link posts, Bluesky treats posts with links the same as text-only. This is the single biggest CTA advantage among all platforms.

Four Feed Types

FeedMechanicsGrowth Relevance
FollowingPure chronological, posts from followed accountsLOW — only reaches existing followers
DiscoverAlgorithmic: engagement velocity, social graph proximity, recency, language detectionHIGH — primary growth vector for new accounts
Custom FeedsThird-party algorithms: keyword, engagement threshold, author list — any logicVERY HIGH — unique to Bluesky, Tim can build his own
Trending TopicsRapid engagement velocity in Explore/SearchMEDIUM — news-cycle dependent
DISCOVER RANKING SIGNALS: ┌─ Engagement velocity (20 likes in 10 min > 50 likes in 6 hrs) ├─ Replies + quote posts weight > likes (conversation = boost) ├─ Social graph proximity (2nd/3rd degree connections) ├─ Content freshness (strong recency bias) ├─ Language detection (matches user preference) └─ Account reputation (follower count, engagement ratio, age)
No Shadowban: Bluesky has no shadowban mechanism. Moderation uses labelers (third-party tagging services), not algorithmic suppression. Content is either visible or removed — no grey zone.

AT Protocol Architecture

User's App (Bluesky client) │ v Feed Generator (any server implementing the spec) │ v App View (Bluesky indexing service) │ v PDS (Personal Data Server — user data lives here) KEY: Tim can build + host his own feed generator → authority positioning

4. AT Protocol Advantages

Data Portability: All posts, follows, and profile stored in your Personal Data Server (PDS). Exportable anytime. If Bluesky fails, your content survives. Investment is protected.
Account Portability: Identity is a DID (Decentralized Identifier). Move between PDS providers without losing followers. Custom domains work as handles.
Open Firehose: All public data available in real-time. Build analytics dashboards on RUVDS. Monitor mentions, engagement, competitor activity for free.
Labeling System: Decentralized moderation through labelers. Future play: build a «Quality AI Content» labeler to tag high-quality posts in the niche.

Domain Handle: The Brand Power Move

Critical Recommendation: Set sborka.work as Bluesky handle instead of timzinin.bsky.social. Every post, reply, and repost shows your domain. It's a verified badge equivalent (proves domain ownership). Free brand awareness with every interaction. The AI/tech community respects custom domain handles.
DOMAIN HANDLE SETUP (15 min): 1. Add DNS TXT record: _atproto.sborka.work → did=did:plc:xxxxx 2. Update handle in Bluesky Settings → Advanced → Change Handle 3. Now every post shows "sborka.work" as the handle Alternative: use timzinin.com for personal brand focus Can be changed later — not permanent

6. Tone of Voice & Cultural Norms

Bluesky's culture was shaped by journalists, academics, developers, and people leaving X. Understanding this is critical for content resonance.

Cultural NormImplication for Tim
Earnestness over ironyGenuine enthusiasm about your work lands better than sarcastic takes
Expertise is respectedTim's real-world experience with AI systems will be valued, not mocked
Self-promo is context-dependent«Check out my product!» = ignored. «I built X, here's what I learned» = welcomed
Alt text is expectedALWAYS add alt text to images. Cultural norm, not optional
Anti-hustle cultureFrame building businesses as craft, not «rise and grind»
Tech literacy is highNo need to over-explain. Audience understands APIs, Docker, agents
What to AVOID:
  • «10X your productivity with AI» — hustle culture / marketing speak
  • Engagement bait («RT if you agree») — seen as manipulative
  • Cross-posting exact Twitter content — feels lazy, different cultural context
  • Selling hard — community resistance to pure marketing
  • Russian language posts — English dominant; Russian audience negligible on Bluesky
  • Ignoring alt text — cultural norm violation
