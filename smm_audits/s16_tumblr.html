1. Algorithm & Content Distribution

Tumblr's distribution is fundamentally different from algorithm-heavy platforms. There is no single powerful algorithmic feed. Content reaches users through four channels:

ChannelMechanicsGrowth Impact
Dashboard (Following)Chronological. «Best Stuff First» exists but most power users disable itMEDIUM — followers actually see your content
Reblog ChainsTHE core mechanic. Each reblog exposes to reblogger's followers. Multi-level chains expand exponentiallyVERY HIGH — 1 reblog by popular account = 10K-100K+ notes
Explore / For YouTrending + recommended. Considers engagement velocity, tag relevance, blog activityHIGH — primary discovery for new users
Tags & SearchFirst 20 tags indexed. Users can follow tags → posts appear on strangers' dashboardsHIGH — tag following is critical discovery channel
Key Rule: Optimize for reblogs, not likes. Likes are vanity metrics on Tumblr. Reblogs ARE distribution. Create content people want to put on their own blog. Posts with commentary added in reblogs perform best because each addition renews the post.
REBLOG CHAIN VIRAL MECHANICS: Tim posts → User A reblogs (adds commentary) → User B reblogs from A (adds more) → User C reblogs from B ... Each level = exponential reach expansion 1 reblog by 50K-follower account = 10,000-100,000+ notes NO equivalent mechanism on LinkedIn or Telegram

The «Tumblr Post» Format: Short, punchy observation in a specific voice. Often absurdist or meta. Example: «the thing about building AI agents is that eventually you realize you're just teaching a computer to procrastinate the same way you do.» This format can reach 50,000+ notes. Study the rhythm, then apply it to AI/tech observations. This is the #1 growth lever.
Growth Hack: Ask Games. Enable anonymous asks. Post «send me your AI hot takes.» Answer each as a separate post. One prompt = 5-10 posts + builds community. Each answer gets reblogged independently.

5. Community Culture & Norms

Tumblr's culture is shaped by anti-corporate sentiment, humor-first ethos, Gen Z dominance (50% of users), and strong creative communities. Understanding this is essential.

EARNS REBLOGS

  • Genuine personality in every post
  • Self-deprecating humor about building AI
  • Admitting failures and messy processes
  • Original infographics/visual content
  • Answering asks thoughtfully
  • Reblogging others with added commentary
  • Anti-hype, realistic takes on AI

GETS YOU IGNORED

  • Corporate language («leveraging synergies»)
  • Screenshots of LinkedIn/Twitter posts
  • Pure promotional content
  • Link-only posts
  • Hustle culture («rise and grind»)
  • One-way broadcasting without engagement
  • AI-generated images without disclosure
Anti-AI-Art Sentiment: Tumblr community has strong feelings against AI-generated art. If using AI images, ALWAYS disclose. Better: use real screenshots, diagrams, infographics. Frame AI work as augmenting humans, not replacing them. «The system is broken, here's how to navigate it» resonates better than «5 tips to optimize your LinkedIn.»

6. Tone of Voice

TIM'S TUMBLR VOICE FORMULA: technical expertise + self-aware humor + genuine enthusiasm + slight chaos energy Words to AVOID: leverage, synergy, hustle, disrupt, link in bio, 10x Words that WORK: honestly, the thing about, turns out, TIL, so apparently

LinkedIn Voice vs Tumblr Voice

LinkedIn (DON'T)Tumblr (DO)
«Proud to announce our revolutionary AI system is live! 6 agents processing 10K items/day.»«so i accidentally deployed 6 AI agents to production at 2am and they've been arguing with each other about formatting ever since. this is fine.»
«5 Tips to Optimize Your AI Career in 2026»«the thing about AI job titles is they change faster than my agents can keep up. 'prompt engineer' lasted like 8 months. we're all just making it up.»
Key Principle: Be a person, not a brand. Lowercase is fine. Show the messy reality. Tumblr rewards authenticity and personality over polish. The «builder who shows their work, including the disasters» voice is unoccupied and perfectly aligned with Tim's brand.
